# Synthesis: Final Assembly & Review

**Dependencies**: All applicable layers must be complete before synthesis (Layers 1-8 for B2C; Layers 1-7 for B2B)
**Produces**: Completed strategy document, Executive Summary, Next Steps with 30/90/180-day priorities, Review Schedule

---

## Why This Step Matters

A strategy document is not a collection of worksheets. It is a coherent narrative: here is who we are, who we serve, what we say, how we plan, where we show up, and what we create. Synthesis is the step where all eight layers are woven together into a document that can be read, understood, and acted upon by anyone on the team — even those who were not in the room.

This is also the quality gate. Before the strategy is finalized, Claude reviews the entire document for coherence, completeness, and actionability. Gaps get flagged. Inconsistencies get resolved. And the user gets a final review before the document is locked.

## Step 1: Coherence Review

Read through all layer outputs in sequence. For each check below, confirm that the strategy hangs together. Flag any item that fails.

### Coherence Checklist

| # | Check | Layers Involved | Status | Notes |

| 3 | The StoryBrand hero's desire (Layer 3) aligns with the segment's primary JTBD (Layer 2) | 2 → 3 | | |
| 4 | The positioning "unlike" clause (Layer 2) is reflected in the brand script's guide authority (Layer 3) and the competitive analysis (Layer 4) | 2 → 3 → 4 | | |
| 5 | SMART objectives (Layer 4) have corresponding KPIs in the Control section (Layer 4) and targets in the RACE funnel (Layer 5) | 4 → 4 → 5 | | |
| 6 | Every high-priority tactic (Layer 4) has a RACE stage assignment (Layer 5) AND a PESO channel assignment (Layer 6) | 4 → 5 → 6 | | |

| 8 | Budget allocations in SOSTAC (Layer 4) align with PESO channel budgets (Layer 6) and content production needs (Layer 7) | 4 → 6 → 7 | | |

### Handling Failures

For any item that fails the coherence check:
1. Identify the source of the inconsistency
2. Propose a resolution to the user
3. Update the relevant sections of the strategy document
4. Re-check the item after the update

## Step 3: Write the Executive Summary

The Executive Summary is the most important section of the strategy document. It must stand alone — someone reading ONLY the executive summary should understand the company's strategic direction, target audiences, key messages, primary tactics, and expected outcomes.

### Executive Summary Structure

Write the Executive Summary using this structure:

```
## Executive Summary

### Purpose
[1-2 sentences: Why this strategy was developed. Reference the scenario and trigger from intake.]

### Who We Are
[2-3 sentences: The Golden Circle — WHY the company exists, HOW it differs, WHAT it offers.
Use the One-Liner from Layer 3 as a concise expression.]

### Who We Serve
[For each target segment, 2-3 sentences: Who they are, what job they need done, and why they
choose us. Reference the positioning statement.]

### What We Say
[2-3 sentences: The core message. The StoryBrand narrative in brief — the customer has a problem,
we are the guide, here is the plan, here is the outcome. Use the messaging hierarchy from Layer 3.]

### What We Will Do
[3-5 bullet points: The strategic bets from Layer 4. The top priorities. The big moves.
Not a tactic list — the strategic direction.]

### Where We Will Show Up
[2-3 sentences: The PESO channel summary — the primary Paid, Earned, Shared, and Owned channels.
Reference the RACE x PESO integration.]

### How We Will Measure Success
[3-5 bullet points: The top-level KPIs from Layer 4. Revenue targets, lead targets,
awareness targets, retention targets. With timeframes.]

### What Happens Next
[2-3 sentences: The immediate next steps — the first 30 days from the action plan.
Reference the 90-day plan.]
```

### Executive Summary Quality Checks

- **Length**: 1-2 pages maximum. If it is longer, it is not a summary.
- **Standalone**: Could someone who has never seen the rest of the document understand the strategy?
- **Actionable**: Does it point toward what happens next?
- **Specific**: Does it contain real numbers, real segment names, real channel names — not generic language?
- **Aligned**: Does it accurately represent the detailed strategy in the document?

Present the Executive Summary to the user for review before finalizing.

## Step 6: Final User Review

Present the completed strategy document to the user for final review. Use this prompt:

---

"The strategy document is now complete. Here is a summary of what we have built:

**Layers Completed**:
- Layer 1: Golden Circle — WHY / HOW / WHAT
- Layer 2: Customer Insight — [N] segments with JTBD, VPC, and Positioning
- Layer 3: StoryBrand — Brand Script(s), One-Liner, Messaging Hierarchy
- Layer 4: SOSTAC — Situation Analysis, SMART Objectives, Strategy, Tactics, 90-Day Plan, KPIs
- Layer 5: RACE — Customer Journey Funnels per segment
- Layer 6: PESO — Channel Mix with RACE integration
- Layer 7: Content Matrix — Four-quadrant content plan with 90-day calendar
- Layer 8: P&G Validation — Cross-check scorecard

**Coherence Review**: [N] of 12 checks passed. [Any items flagged and resolved.]
**Completeness Review**: [N] of 18 items present. [Any gaps noted.]

Please review the document and let me know:
1. Is there anything that feels inaccurate or misaligned with your understanding?
2. Are there areas where you want more detail?
3. Are there areas where we should simplify?
4. Are the next steps clear and actionable?
5. Is there anything missing that you expected to see?"

## Step 7: Save the Completed Document

Once the user approves the final document:

1. **Save the strategy document** to: `Strategy/Output/[Company-Name]-marketing-strategy-[date].md`
   - Use the company name from intake (lowercase, hyphenated)
   - Use the current date in YYYY-MM-DD format
   - Example: `Strategy/Output/acme-corp-marketing-strategy-2026-03-01.md`

2. **Update the Strategy State** in the document:
   ```
   ## Strategy State

   **Status**: COMPLETE
   **Completed**: [date]
   **Scenario**: [scenario from intake]
   **Layers Completed**: All (1-8 + Synthesis)
   **Next Review**: [date from review schedule]
   ```

3. **Optionally copy the Executive Summary** to a separate file:
   - `Strategy/Output/[Company-Name]-executive-summary-[date].md`
   - This is useful when the full strategy document is large and stakeholders only need the summary
   - Ask the user: "Would you like the Executive Summary saved as a separate file for easy sharing?"

## Session Wrap-Up

End the session with:

"Your marketing strategy is complete and saved to `Strategy/Output/[filename]`.

**What you have**:
- A complete marketing strategy document covering purpose, audience, messaging, planning, journey mapping, channel strategy, and content planning
- An Executive Summary that stands alone for stakeholder presentation
- A 90-day action plan with clear owners and deadlines
- A content calendar for the first quarter
- A KPI framework and review schedule

**What to do next**:
1. Share the Executive Summary with key stakeholders for alignment
2. Begin the 30-day priority actions immediately
3. Schedule the first weekly metrics check
4. Return to this document at each quarterly review to assess progress and update the strategy

If you need to update or revise any section, you can resume a session and Claude will pick up where you left off using the Strategy State in the document.

Good luck with the execution."
