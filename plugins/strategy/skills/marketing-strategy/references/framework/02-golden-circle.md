# Layer 1: Purpose & Identity — Golden Circle

**Framework Source**: Simon Sinek's Golden Circle (Start With Why)
**Dependencies**: None (this is the foundational layer)
**Produces**: WHY statement, HOW differentiators, WHAT offerings, Golden Circle Statement

---

## Why This Layer Matters

The Golden Circle is based on a biological principle: the WHY communicates with the limbic brain (trust, loyalty, decision-making) while the WHAT communicates with the neocortex (rational analysis). Companies that start with WHY create emotional connection before rational justification. This is why people don't buy WHAT you do — they buy WHY you do it.

Every subsequent layer in this framework references the WHY, HOW, and WHAT. Getting this right — and being honest rather than aspirational — is critical.

---

## Questions

Ask these conversationally, one section at a time. Do NOT dump all questions at once.

### WHY — Purpose, Cause, or Belief

The WHY is not about making money. Money is a result. The WHY is the reason the company exists — the cause that drives everything.

1. **Why does your company exist beyond making money?** What change do you want to see in the world, your industry, or your customers' lives?
2. **If your company disappeared tomorrow, what would the world lose?** What gap would exist?
3. **Complete this sentence: "We believe that ___"** (This should feel like a conviction, not a tagline)
4. **What was the founding insight or frustration that started this company?** What problem did the founder(s) see that they couldn't ignore?

#### Follow-Up Probes (use when answers are vague)

- If the answer sounds like a mission statement from a corporate website: "That sounds like what you do. But *why* does that matter? Who cares, and why should they?"
- If the answer is too broad ("to help people"): "What kind of people? Help them with what specifically? Why does that particular help matter more than anything else you could do?"
- If the answer is about the product: "That's the WHAT — the product. Let's go deeper. Why did you build *that* product? What belief drove that choice?"
- If the user is stuck: "Think about a moment when you felt most proud of your company. What was happening? What did it mean? That feeling usually points toward the WHY."

### HOW — Differentiating Process and Values in Action

The HOW is how the company brings its WHY to life. These are the specific actions, values, and principles that differentiate the company.

1. **How do you do what you do differently from everyone else in your space?** What is your approach, method, or philosophy?
2. **What are your 3-5 core values?** Not aspirational values — actual values that show up in day-to-day operations. For each one: give a specific example of that value in action.
3. **What would a customer say is different about working with you vs. a competitor?** (If you don't know, what do you *want* them to say?)
4. **What is your "unfair advantage"?** The thing that is genuinely hard for others to replicate — proprietary technology, unique expertise, network effects, cultural DNA, etc.

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If values are generic ("innovation", "excellence"): "Every company claims those. What does [value] *actually look like* in your company? Give me a specific story or decision where that value determined the outcome."
- If the user can't articulate differentiation: "Imagine a customer is choosing between you and your closest competitor. Price is the same. What tips the decision in your favor?"

### WHAT — Products, Services, and Proof

The WHAT is the tangible expression of the WHY and HOW. It's the easiest to articulate but the least differentiating.

1. **What tangible products or services do you offer?** List the main ones.
2. **For each major offering, what is the key outcome it delivers for the customer?** (Not features — outcomes)
3. **How do your offerings connect back to your WHY?** (This is the coherence check — if the WHAT doesn't serve the WHY, something is misaligned)

---

## Artifact: Golden Circle Statement

After gathering answers, draft a Golden Circle Statement in this format:

```
WHY:  We believe that [belief/purpose].
HOW:  We [differentiating approach/method/values in action].
WHAT: We [products/services and the outcomes they deliver].
```

**Validation**: Present the statement and ask:
- "Does this feel authentic? Not aspirational — but genuinely true today?"
- "Would your team rally behind this? Would they recognize it as your company?"
- "Could a competitor say the exact same thing? If yes, we need to sharpen the HOW."

**Iterate** until the user confirms the statement feels both true and differentiated.

---

## Connecting Forward

The Golden Circle Statement feeds directly into:
- **Layer 2 (Customer Insight)**: The WHAT tells us who needs it. The WHY tells us what kind of relationship we build.
- **Layer 3 (StoryBrand)**: The WHY becomes the guide's authority. The HOW becomes the plan.
- **Layer 4 (SOSTAC)**: The WHY and WHAT define the strategic direction.
- **All layers**: The WHY is the North Star for every decision downstream.
