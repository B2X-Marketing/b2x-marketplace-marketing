# Layer 4: Strategic Planning — SOSTAC

**Framework Source**: PR Smith's SOSTAC Planning Framework
**Dependencies**: Layers 1-3 (Golden Circle, Customer Insight, StoryBrand) — uses WHY/HOW/WHAT, target segments, positioning statements, and brand scripts to build a coherent marketing plan
**Produces**: Situation Analysis, SMART Objectives, Strategy Summary, Tactics Overview, 90-Day Action Plan, Control/KPI Framework

---

## Why This Layer Matters

Strategy without structure is just a list of good ideas. SOSTAC provides the structure: Where are we now? Where do we want to go? How do we get there? What do we do day-to-day? And how do we know it's working?

SOSTAC is one of the most widely used marketing planning frameworks in the world because it forces linear thinking — each component feeds the next. You cannot set meaningful objectives without understanding your situation. You cannot define tactics without a strategic direction. And without control mechanisms, you are spending money on faith.

This layer bridges the "who we are and who we serve" work of Layers 1-3 with the "what we actually do" work of Layers 5-7.

## S — Situation Analysis

**Goal**: Establish an honest baseline of where marketing stands today. This is a marketing-specific situation analysis, not a general business assessment.

Ask these questions conversationally, one block at a time.

### Current Marketing Performance

1. **How would you describe your current level of marketing maturity?**
   - No marketing (word-of-mouth only)
   - Basic (website + maybe one channel)
   - Developing (multiple channels, some tracking)
   - Established (documented processes, regular reporting)
   - Advanced (data-driven, automated, optimized)
2. **What marketing activities are you currently running?** Walk me through a typical month.
3. **Do you have analytics in place?** If yes, share what you can:
   - Website traffic (monthly visitors)
   - Conversion rates (visitor-to-lead, lead-to-customer)
   - Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
   - Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
   - Email list size and engagement rates
   - Social media following and engagement
4. **What has worked well in the past?** Be specific — which campaigns, channels, or tactics produced real results?
5. **What has failed or underperformed?** What did you try that didn't work? Why do you think it didn't?

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If the user says "nothing is working": "Let's be more precise. Where are your current customers coming from today? Whatever path they took — that is working, even if it's not intentional."
- If no analytics exist: "That's okay — we'll flag analytics setup as a priority action. For now, estimate what you can: roughly how many leads do you get per month? How many become customers?"
- If the user is vague about past results: "Think about the last marketing investment that felt like a win. What was it? And the last one that felt like wasted money. What happened?"

### Marketing-Specific SWOT

> **SWOT Skill available**: For a comprehensive SWOT analysis with structured data output, visual matrix, and optional strategic actions, invoke the SWOT skill at `Strategy/.claude/skills/swot/SKILL.md` in `strategy` mode. It uses Layers 1-3 context to auto-generate a marketing-scoped SWOT with evidence tied to strategy artifacts. The questions below serve as fallback guidance for conversational discovery.

**Important**: This is NOT a general business SWOT. Focus exclusively on marketing and communications.

1. **Strengths**: What marketing advantages do you have? Consider:
   - Strong brand recognition or reputation
   - Existing customer relationships or email list
   - Content library or thought leadership
   - Unique data, case studies, or social proof
   - Team expertise or partnerships
   - Technology or tool advantages
2. **Weaknesses**: Where are the marketing gaps? Consider:
   - Lack of brand awareness
   - Inconsistent messaging or positioning
   - Weak digital presence (website, SEO, social)
   - No marketing automation or CRM
   - Limited content or creative resources
   - Analytics/tracking gaps
3. **Opportunities**: What external factors could you capitalize on? Consider:
   - Market trends shifting in your favor
   - Competitor weaknesses or gaps
   - Emerging channels or platforms
   - Regulatory or industry changes
   - Partnership or co-marketing opportunities
   - Underserved audience segments
4. **Threats**: What external factors could undermine your marketing? Consider:
   - Competitor marketing investment or positioning
   - Market saturation or commoditization
   - Platform algorithm changes (social, search)
   - Rising acquisition costs
   - Regulatory restrictions on marketing/data
   - Economic conditions affecting customer spend

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If strengths are all product-related: "Those are product strengths. What about marketing specifically — your ability to communicate, reach, and convert your audience?"
- If the SWOT is too generic: "Let's make this actionable. For each item, ask: so what? What does this mean for what we should do next?"

### Competitor Analysis

1. **Who are your top 3-5 marketing competitors?** These may differ from your business competitors — focus on companies competing for the same audience's attention.
2. **For each competitor, assess:**
   - What appears to be their WHY or positioning? What story are they telling?
   - What channels are they most active on?
   - What seems to be working for them? (High engagement, strong SEO presence, visible ad spend, PR coverage)
   - Where do they appear weak or absent?
   - How does their messaging compare to your brand script from Layer 3?
3. **Where is the whitespace?** What is no competitor doing well — or at all — that represents an opportunity for you?

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If the user claims no competitors: "Every company has competitors for attention, even if not direct product competitors. Who else is talking to your target segment? What content are they consuming instead of yours?"
- If competitor analysis is surface-level: "Go to their website. What is their headline? Their CTA? Their lead magnet? That tells you their strategy. How does it compare to ours?"

## O — Objectives (SMART)

**Goal**: Set specific, measurable objectives per target segment. Every objective must pass the SMART test: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.

**Key concept to explain to the user**: Objectives are not "grow revenue" or "increase brand awareness." Those are aspirations. An objective is: "Increase qualified leads from Segment A by 30% (from 50 to 65 per month) within 6 months, measured via CRM pipeline reports."

### Objective Setting per Segment

For each priority segment from Layer 2:

1. **Revenue/Growth Objectives**:
   - What is the revenue target for this segment over the next 12 months?
   - How many new customers do you need from this segment?
   - What is the target average deal size or transaction value?

2. **Lead Generation Objectives**:
   - How many leads per month do you need to hit the revenue target? (Work backwards: target revenue / deal size = customers needed / conversion rate = leads needed)
   - What qualifies as a "lead" for this segment? (Form fill, demo request, phone call, etc.)

3. **Brand Awareness Objectives**:
   - What is the target reach (impressions, website visitors, social followers)?
   - How will you measure awareness? (Search volume for brand terms, direct traffic, aided/unaided recall)

4. **Retention/Engagement Objectives**:
   - What is the current customer retention rate?
   - What is the target retention rate?
   - What is the target for repeat purchases, upsells, or referrals?

### Express Objectives per RACE Stage

Map each objective to the RACE funnel stage it belongs to (this connects forward to Layer 5):

| RACE Stage | Objective | Current Baseline | Target | Timeframe |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|--------|-----------|
| **Reach** | [awareness/traffic objective] | [current] | [target] | [date] |
| **Act** | [engagement/lead objective] | [current] | [target] | [date] |
| **Convert** | [sales/revenue objective] | [current] | [target] | [date] |
| **Engage** | [retention/advocacy objective] | [current] | [target] | [date] |

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If objectives are not measurable: "How will you know if you've achieved this? What number changes? Where do you see that number?"
- If objectives are unrealistic: "Let's reality-check. You're at [X] today and want to reach [Y] in [timeframe]. That's a [Z]% increase. What specifically would change to produce that result?"
- If the user cannot set baselines: "That's a gap we need to close. Let's set 'establish baseline measurement' as a 30-day action item, and set provisional targets based on industry benchmarks."
- If objectives are all acquisition-focused: "What about your existing customers? Retention is usually cheaper than acquisition. Should we set targets for customer lifetime value or repeat purchase rate?"

## S — Strategy

**Goal**: Define the strategic direction — a high-level summary of how you will achieve the objectives. Strategy is NOT tactics. Strategy is the direction; tactics are the vehicles.

1. **Positioning Summary**: Restate the positioning from Layer 2 for each target segment. This is the strategic foundation — all tactics must reinforce this position.

2. **Priority Segments**: Confirm the segment priority order from Layer 2. For each:
   - What percentage of marketing effort/budget goes to this segment?
   - Is this segment primarily an acquisition play or a retention/growth play?

3. **Messaging Approach**: Reference the brand scripts from Layer 3.
   - Which elements of the brand script are most critical for each stage of the funnel?
   - What is the primary emotional lever for this segment? (Fear of failure, aspiration, social proof, urgency)

4. **Strategic Direction — Acquire vs. Grow**:
   - What is the balance between acquiring new customers and growing existing accounts?
   - For acquisition: What channels are most efficient for reaching your target segments?
   - For growth: What upsell, cross-sell, or expansion opportunities exist?

5. **Strategic Bets**: What are the 2-3 big bets this strategy makes?
   - Where are you concentrating resources?
   - What are you deliberately choosing NOT to do?
   - What would need to be true for this strategy to work?

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If the strategy sounds like a tactic list: "That's what we'll do — but let's first define the direction. Are we primarily trying to be the known expert, the low-cost leader, the premium option, the disruptor? That direction shapes which tactics make sense."
- If acquisition vs. growth is not addressed: "A dollar spent retaining an existing customer typically returns more than a dollar spent acquiring a new one. Where is the biggest revenue opportunity right now — new logos or existing accounts?"

## T — Tactics

**Goal**: Identify the high-level tactics that will execute the strategy. Detailed tactical planning happens in Layers 5-7 (RACE journey, PESO channels, Content Matrix). Here we establish the tactical landscape.

1. **Tactics Inventory**: Based on the strategy above, what high-level tactics will you employ? Check all that apply and indicate priority (High / Medium / Low):

   | Tactic | Priority | Notes |
   |--------|----------|-------|
   | SEO / Organic search | | |
   | Content marketing (blog, articles, guides) | | |
   | Email marketing / nurture sequences | | |
   | Social media (organic) | | |
   | Social media (paid) | | |
   | PPC / Search ads | | |
   | Display / Programmatic advertising | | |
   | Video marketing | | |
   | Webinars / Virtual events | | |
   | Podcasting | | |
   | PR / Media relations | | |
   | Influencer / Partner marketing | | |
   | Referral / Affiliate programs | | |
   | Events / Trade shows | | |
   | Direct outreach / ABM | | |
   | Community building | | |
   | Other: [specify] | | |

2. **Budget Allocation**: Given the budget from intake:
   - What percentage goes to paid vs. organic/owned tactics?
   - What is the split across top-of-funnel (awareness) vs. bottom-of-funnel (conversion)?
   - Are there minimum spends required to be effective on any channel? (e.g., PPC typically requires a threshold to generate meaningful data)

3. **Team Capabilities**:
   - Which of these tactics can you execute in-house?
   - Which require external support (agency, freelancer, contractor)?
   - What skill gaps need to be filled? What is the plan to fill them?
   - What is the realistic capacity of your team? (Hours per week dedicated to marketing)

4. **Technology Stack**:
   - What tools and platforms do you need? (CRM, email platform, analytics, social scheduling, ad platforms, etc.)
   - What do you already have vs. what needs to be added?
   - What is the budget for marketing technology?

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If everything is marked high priority: "You can't do everything well at once. If you could only do THREE of these tactics in the next 90 days, which three would move the needle most?"
- If budget and tactics are mismatched: "The tactics you've listed would typically require [estimate]. With your current budget, we should focus on [subset]. Which ones give you the best return for the investment?"
- If everything is outsourced: "Who owns the relationship with the customer? If all marketing is outsourced, how do you maintain brand voice and strategic direction?"

## A — Actions (90-Day Plan)

**Goal**: Convert strategy and tactics into a concrete 90-day action plan. Ninety days is long enough to see results but short enough to maintain urgency.

1. **What are the top 3-5 priorities for the next 90 days?** For each:
   - What specifically needs to happen?
   - Who owns it? (Name or role)
   - What resources or tools are needed?
   - What is the deadline?
   - What does "done" look like?

2. **Sequence these priorities**: Which must happen first? Are there dependencies?

3. **Quick Wins**: What can be accomplished in the first 2 weeks that would create early momentum or visible results?

### 90-Day Action Plan Template

| Priority | Action | Owner | Tools/Resources | Deadline | Done Criteria |
|----------|--------|-------|----------------|----------|---------------|
| 1 | [action] | [who] | [what's needed] | [date] | [definition of done] |
| 2 | [action] | [who] | [what's needed] | [date] | [definition of done] |
| 3 | [action] | [who] | [what's needed] | [date] | [definition of done] |
| 4 | [action] | [who] | [what's needed] | [date] | [definition of done] |
| 5 | [action] | [who] | [what's needed] | [date] | [definition of done] |

### Week-by-Week Milestones

| Week | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| Week 1-2 | Quick wins: [list] |
| Week 3-4 | Foundation: [list] |
| Week 5-8 | Build: [list] |
| Week 9-12 | Optimize: [list] |

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If no one owns a priority: "Who specifically is responsible for making this happen? If the answer is 'the team' or 'everyone,' it will be no one. Assign a single owner."
- If actions are too vague: "Make it concrete. Instead of 'improve social media,' try 'publish 3 LinkedIn posts per week targeting Segment A pain points, using the messaging from our brand script.'"
- If there are too many priorities: "If everything is a priority, nothing is. What is the ONE action that, if completed, would have the biggest impact on your objectives?"

## C — Control

**Goal**: Define how you will measure progress, report results, and make decisions based on data.

### KPI Framework

For each SMART objective set in the Objectives section, define a corresponding KPI:

| Objective | KPI | Data Source | Current Baseline | Target | Reporting Frequency |
|-----------|-----|------------|-----------------|--------|-------------------|
| [objective 1] | [metric] | [where it's tracked] | [current value] | [target value] | [weekly/monthly/quarterly] |
| [objective 2] | [metric] | [where it's tracked] | [current value] | [target value] | [weekly/monthly/quarterly] |

### Reporting Cadence

1. **How often will you review marketing performance?**
   - Weekly: Quick metrics check (traffic, leads, spend)
   - Monthly: Full performance review against objectives
   - Quarterly: Strategic review — are we on track? Do we need to adjust?

2. **Who receives the reports?** (Marketing team, leadership, board, stakeholders from intake)

3. **What format?** (Dashboard, written report, meeting deck)

### Decision Triggers

Define the conditions that trigger a change in approach:

1. **Scale trigger**: "If [metric] exceeds target by [X]%, increase budget/effort by [Y]%"
2. **Pivot trigger**: "If [metric] is below target by [X]% for [Y] consecutive weeks/months, [specific action: pause, reallocate, redesign]"
3. **Kill trigger**: "If [tactic/channel] does not achieve [minimum threshold] within [timeframe], shut it down and reallocate to [alternative]"
4. **Opportunity trigger**: "If [signal: competitor exits, viral content, market shift], we will [response plan]"

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If no KPIs are defined: "What number would you check to know if marketing is working? That is your first KPI."
- If reporting cadence is unclear: "Who is going to look at these numbers and how often? If no one looks, no one adjusts. What cadence is realistic for your team?"
- If decision triggers are missing: "Knowing what to measure is step one. Step two is deciding in advance what you'll do with the data. At what point would you stop doing something that isn't working?"
