# Layer 5: Customer Journey — RACE

**Framework Source**: Dave Chaffey's RACE Digital Marketing Framework
**Dependencies**: Layer 4 (SOSTAC objectives and tactics) and Layer 2 (target segments with JTBD and positioning)
**Produces**: RACE Funnel Map per primary segment — with tactics, targets, and KPIs for each stage

---

## Why This Layer Matters

SOSTAC (Layer 4) defined what you want to achieve and the high-level approach. RACE turns that into a customer-centric journey: how a stranger becomes aware of you, interacts with you, buys from you, and becomes a loyal advocate.

Without a mapped journey, marketing activities happen in isolation — a blog post here, an ad there, an email sometimes. RACE connects the dots so that every tactic has a clear role in moving the customer from one stage to the next.

The framework uses four stages: Reach (grow your audience), Act (encourage interaction), Convert (drive sales), Engage (build loyalty). Each stage has its own goals, tactics, content types, and KPIs. And because different segments take different paths, you build one RACE funnel per primary segment.

---

## Prerequisites Check

Before starting this layer, confirm you have from previous layers:
- **From Layer 2**: Target segment profiles (demographics, psychographics, JTBD), Positioning Statements, Segment Priority Matrix
- **From Layer 3**: Brand Scripts (hero's desire, problem, guide, plan, CTA, failure, success), One-Liner, Messaging Hierarchy

## How to Work Through This Layer

Build one complete RACE funnel per primary segment before moving to the next segment. For each segment:

1. Start with Reach, work through to Engage
2. Reference the segment's JTBD and brand script throughout
3. Connect each stage's tactics to the SOSTAC objectives and 90-day plan
4. Set targets and KPIs that roll up to the SOSTAC objectives

---

## R — Reach (Grow Your Audience)

**Goal**: Build awareness among your target segment. Get found by the right people. This is the top of the funnel — moving strangers to visitors.

### Questions

Ask these for each primary segment:

1. **Where does [Segment Name] discover new solutions, products, or services?** Think about:
   - Search engines (what do they search for?)
   - Social platforms (which ones? how do they use them?)
   - Industry publications, blogs, or media
   - Conferences, events, or trade shows
   - Word of mouth, referrals, peer recommendations
   - Online communities (Reddit, Slack groups, forums, LinkedIn groups)
   - Podcasts, YouTube channels, newsletters they follow

2. **What are the key search terms this segment uses?**
   - Awareness-stage terms (problem-aware but not solution-aware): "How to [solve problem]", "Why is [symptom] happening"
   - Consideration-stage terms (solution-aware): "[Category] software", "Best [solution type] for [segment]"
   - Decision-stage terms (brand-aware): "[Your company name]", "[Your company] vs [competitor]", "[Your company] reviews"

3. **Which discovery channels are most viable for you right now?** Consider:
   - Budget available (from Layer 4)
   - Team capability (from Layer 4)
   - Competitive intensity on each channel
   - Time to results (SEO is slow, PPC is fast, content compounds over time)

4. **What is your target reach per channel?** Be specific:
   - Monthly website visitors from organic search
   - Monthly impressions or reach from social
   - Monthly impressions from paid ads
   - Email list growth rate

5. **What is the target cost-per-reach?** What can you afford to spend to get one person from your target segment to your website or content?
   - CPM (cost per thousand impressions) target
   - CPC (cost per click) target
   - Is there a channel where reach is essentially free? (Organic social, SEO, partnerships)

### Reach Tactics Checklist

For each channel selected, define:

| Channel | Tactic | Target Metric | Current Baseline | 90-Day Target | Budget |
|---------|--------|--------------|-----------------|---------------|--------|
| SEO | [specific actions] | Organic visitors/mo | [current] | [target] | [spend] |
| PPC | [specific actions] | Paid visitors/mo | [current] | [target] | [spend] |
| Social (organic) | [specific actions] | Impressions/mo | [current] | [target] | [spend] |
| Social (paid) | [specific actions] | Reach/mo | [current] | [target] | [spend] |
| Content/PR | [specific actions] | Referral visitors/mo | [current] | [target] | [spend] |

| Other | [specific actions] | [metric] | [current] | [target] | [spend] |

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If the user wants to be "everywhere": "Being everywhere at once means being mediocre everywhere. Which 2-3 channels give you the highest concentration of [Segment Name]? Start there and expand once those are working."
- If all reach is paid: "Paid reach stops when the budget stops. What owned or earned channels can you build so that reach compounds over time? SEO and content are investments, not expenses."
- If search terms are unknown: "Put yourself in your customer's shoes. They have the problem from your brand script. What do they type into Google at 11 PM when they're frustrated? That's your starting keyword list."
- If the user cannot estimate targets: "Start with reverse engineering. Your Layer 4 objective says you need [X] leads per month. If your reach-to-act conversion rate is [Y]%, you need [Z] visitors. That's your reach target."

## A — Act (Encourage Interaction)

**Goal**: Convert visitors into prospects. Get them to take a first meaningful action — a micro-conversion that indicates interest. This is the stage where a stranger becomes a known contact.

### Questions

1. **What is the first meaningful interaction you want from [Segment Name]?** The action that signals genuine interest:
   - Downloading a resource (guide, template, checklist, whitepaper)
   - Signing up for a newsletter or email list
   - Watching a video or webinar
   - Taking a quiz or assessment
   - Starting a free trial
   - Requesting a consultation or demo
   - Engaging with a chatbot or interactive tool

2. **What content or experience converts a visitor into a prospect?**
   - What do they need to see, read, or experience to think "this company understands my problem"?
   - Reference the brand script from Layer 3: the guide's empathy and authority should be visible at this stage
   - What content format does this segment prefer? (Written, video, audio, interactive)

3. **What is your lead magnet or transitional CTA?** (Connect to Layer 3, SB7 Element 5)
   - This is the transitional CTA from your brand script — the lower-commitment offer
   - Does it directly address one of the segment's pains or jobs from Layer 2?
   - Is it genuinely valuable on its own (not a thinly-disguised sales pitch)?
   - What format? (PDF guide, video series, email course, tool, template, quiz)

4. **What does the website experience look like for this segment?**
   - Is there a dedicated landing page for this segment?
   - Does the messaging on the page match the brand script hero's desire and problem?
   - Is the CTA clear, specific, and above the fold?
   - Is the page optimized for the channel that drives traffic to it? (Ad → landing page consistency)
   - What is the current bounce rate? Time on page?

5. **What is your target conversion rate from Reach to Act?**
   - Industry benchmark for website visitor-to-lead: 2-5% (varies widely by industry and offer)
   - What is your current rate?
   - What is the target rate?
   - How many leads per month does this produce given your reach targets?

| [lead magnet 2] | [format] | [pain] | [placement] | [%] |

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If there is no lead magnet: "Most visitors will not buy on the first visit. What can you offer that's valuable enough to earn their email address? Think about the brand script — what 'plan' or 'quick win' could you give away that demonstrates your expertise?"
- If the lead magnet is weak: "Would you download this yourself? Is it something you'd share with a colleague? If not, it's not compelling enough. What piece of your expertise would genuinely help them — even if they never buy from you?"
- If the website experience is unclear: "Walk me through what happens when someone from [Segment Name] lands on your homepage. What do they see first? What do they click? Where do they go? At which point do they either engage or leave?"
- If conversion targets are missing: "You said you need [X] leads from Layer 4 objectives. Your reach target is [Y] visitors. That means you need a [X/Y]% conversion rate. Is that realistic? If not, we need to adjust either the reach target or the lead generation approach."

## C — Convert (Drive Sales)

**Goal**: Turn prospects into paying customers. This is the stage where interest becomes revenue — the most scrutinized part of any marketing funnel.

### Questions

1. **What is the conversion path from lead to customer for [Segment Name]?** Map every step:
   - What happens after they download the lead magnet or take the first action?
   - How many touchpoints typically occur between first contact and purchase?
   - Is there a sales handoff? At what point? (Marketing-qualified lead → sales-qualified lead → opportunity → close)
   - Is the purchase self-service (e-commerce, SaaS signup) or sales-assisted (demo, proposal, negotiation)?

2. **What is the typical sales cycle length?**
   - Days, weeks, or months from first contact to close?
   - Does this vary by segment or deal size?
   - What lengthens the cycle? (Committee decisions, procurement, budget cycles, legal review)

3. **What touchpoints happen between interest and purchase?**
   - Email nurture sequences: how many emails? Over what period? What content?
   - Sales calls or demos: how many? Who conducts them?
   - Proposals or quotes: standard or custom?
   - Case studies, testimonials, or references: at what stage are these shared?
   - Free trials, pilots, or proof-of-concept: offered or not?

4. **What are the common objections, and how do you handle them?**
   - Price objections: how do you justify value?
   - Trust objections: what proof do you provide? (Reference guide's authority from Layer 3)
   - Timing objections: why now instead of later? (Reference failure/stakes from Layer 3)
   - Comparison objections: how do you differentiate? (Reference positioning from Layer 2)
   - Internal buy-in objections: how do you help the champion sell internally?

5. **What is the target conversion rate from Act to Convert?**
   - Lead-to-customer conversion rate: what is it today?
   - What is the target?
   - Where do most leads drop off in the funnel? What is the bottleneck?

### Convert Stage Funnel Map

| Funnel Step | Action | Content/Collateral | Owner | Target Conversion Rate |
|-------------|--------|-------------------|-------|----------------------|
| Lead captured | [auto-response / welcome email] | [content] | [marketing/sales] | — |
| Nurture | [email sequence / retargeting] | [content] | [marketing] | [%] to next step |
| Sales engagement | [call / demo / consultation] | [deck / demo script] | [sales] | [%] to next step |
| Proposal | [quote / proposal / trial] | [proposal template] | [sales] | [%] to next step |
| Close | [contract / purchase] | [onboarding materials] | [sales] | [%] overall lead→close |

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If the conversion path is undefined: "Right now, what happens after someone fills out a form or downloads something from your website? If the answer is 'nothing' — that is the biggest gap in your funnel. Every lead needs a next step."
- If there is no nurture sequence: "Not every lead is ready to buy today. How do you stay in front of them until they are? An email nurture sequence is the minimum — it keeps you relevant without requiring manual effort."
- If objection handling is ad hoc: "The same objections come up repeatedly. Let's document the top 5 and prepare responses. This turns your sales process from art into system."
- If the sales cycle is long: "What can you do to shorten it? Common accelerators: social proof (case studies), risk reversal (guarantees, trials), urgency (limited availability, pricing deadlines), and enabling the internal champion (ROI calculators, executive summaries)."
- If there is no clear handoff: "At what point does a lead stop being marketing's responsibility and start being sales' responsibility? If this is undefined, leads fall through the cracks. Define the criteria for a marketing-qualified lead vs. a sales-qualified lead."

## E — Engage (Build Loyalty)

**Goal**: Turn customers into repeat buyers and advocates. This is the most neglected stage and often the most profitable. Retaining an existing customer costs 5-7x less than acquiring a new one.

### Questions

1. **What is the post-purchase experience for [Segment Name]?**
   - What happens immediately after the purchase? (Welcome email, onboarding, setup)
   - Is there a structured onboarding process? How long does it take?
   - When does the customer first experience the value they were promised? (Time-to-value)
   - What is the biggest risk of churn in the first 30/60/90 days?

2. **How do you nurture the ongoing relationship?**
   - Regular communication: email newsletter, product updates, tips, educational content
   - Check-ins: scheduled reviews, success calls, surveys
   - Community: user group, forum, events, Slack/Discord community
   - Exclusive content or offers for existing customers

3. **Do you have loyalty, retention, or expansion programs?**
   - Loyalty rewards or points programs
   - Subscription or retainer models
   - Upsell / cross-sell motions: what triggers them?
   - Renewal process: automated or manual? When does it start?
   - Win-back campaigns for churned customers

4. **How do you turn customers into advocates?**
   - Referral programs: do you have one? What is the incentive?
   - Review/testimonial collection: when and how do you ask?
   - Case study development: process for identifying and producing them
   - Social sharing: do customers share their experience? How do you encourage it?
   - Net Promoter Score (NPS): do you measure it? What is it?

5. **What are the retention and advocacy targets?**
   - Customer retention rate: current vs. target
   - Customer lifetime value (LTV): current vs. target
   - Referral rate: what percentage of new customers come from referrals?
   - NPS: current vs. target
   - Repeat purchase rate (if applicable)

| Touchpoint | Timing | Content/Action | Owner | Success Metric |
|------------|--------|---------------|-------|---------------|
| Welcome/onboarding | Day 0-7 | [onboarding sequence] | [team] | [activation rate] |
| First value delivery | Day [X] | [milestone] | [team] | [time-to-value] |
| Relationship nurture | Ongoing | [email/content cadence] | [team] | [engagement rate] |
| Success check-in | [frequency] | [review/call] | [team] | [satisfaction score] |
| Upsell/cross-sell | [trigger] | [offer] | [team] | [expansion revenue] |
| Referral ask | [trigger] | [referral program] | [team] | [referral rate] |
| Renewal | [timing] | [renewal process] | [team] | [retention rate] |

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If post-purchase experience is absent: "The moment after purchase is when the customer is most engaged and most vulnerable. If they feel ignored, buyer's remorse sets in. What is the first email they receive? What does day one look like?"
- If there is no referral program: "Your happiest customers are your best marketing channel. What would you need to do to make it easy — and rewarding — for them to refer others? Even a simple 'Who else should know about this?' email can work."
- If retention is not tracked: "Do you know how many customers from 12 months ago are still active today? If not, that is a critical blind spot. You could be growing at the top of the funnel while leaking at the bottom."
- If engagement is all product-focused: "Engagement isn't just about using the product. It's about the relationship. What value can you provide beyond the product — content, community, connections, recognition — that makes them feel part of something?"

## Connecting Forward

Layer 5 outputs feed directly into:
- **Layer 6 (PESO)**: The tactics identified in each RACE stage need channel assignments. PESO distributes them across Paid, Earned, Shared, and Owned media. The Reach stage maps heavily to Paid and Earned; Act and Convert map to Owned; Engage maps to Shared and Owned.
- **Layer 7 (Content Matrix)**: Each RACE stage requires different content types. The Content Matrix maps content across the journey — awareness content for Reach, educational content for Act, proof content for Convert, relationship content for Engage.
- **Layer 8 (P&G Validation)**: The RACE funnel is validated against WHO/WHAT/HOW — does the journey serve the right audience, with the right message, through the right mechanism?
