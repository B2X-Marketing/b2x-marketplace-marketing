# Glossary

Shared terminology used across all framework layers. Reference this when a term is ambiguous.

> **Note**: These terms are used internally by the framework. Client-facing deliverables use plain language. Do not include framework brand names, author names, or unexplained acronyms in output documents.

---

## Golden Circle (Layer 1)

- **WHY**: The purpose, cause, or belief that drives the company. Not about making money — it's the reason the company exists. Communicates with the limbic brain (trust, loyalty).
- **HOW**: The differentiating actions, values, and processes that bring the WHY to life. The specific way the company operates differently.
- **WHAT**: The tangible products or services the company offers. The easiest to articulate but least differentiating.

## Customer Insight (Layer 2)

- **JTBD (Jobs-to-be-Done)**: The underlying job a customer is trying to accomplish when they "hire" a product or service. Has three dimensions: functional (practical task), emotional (how they want to feel), social (how they want to be perceived).
- **Value Proposition Canvas (VPC)**: A tool mapping customer needs (jobs, pains, gains) against company offerings (products, pain relievers, gain creators) to assess product-market fit.
- **STP**: Segmentation (dividing the market into groups), Targeting (choosing which groups to prioritize), Positioning (defining how you want to be perceived vs. competitors).
- **Pains**: Things that annoy, frustrate, or block the customer from getting their job done. Includes risks, obstacles, and costs.
- **Gains**: Outcomes the customer would love to achieve beyond just solving the immediate problem. Includes delights, savings, and status improvements.
- **Pain Relievers**: Specific ways your offering addresses customer pains.
- **Gain Creators**: Specific ways your offering creates customer gains.
- **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**: A description of the company or person who gets the most value from your offering and is most likely to buy.

## StoryBrand SB7 (Layer 3)

- **Hero**: The customer — not the company. The person with a problem who needs a guide.
- **Guide**: The company. Positioned as a wise mentor with empathy (understands the struggle) and authority (has the expertise to help).
- **Villain**: The root cause of the hero's problem. Not a person — a condition, force, or frustration (complexity, inefficiency, information overload, etc.).
- **External Problem**: The tangible, surface-level issue the customer faces.
- **Internal Problem**: How the external problem makes the customer feel (frustrated, overwhelmed, incompetent).
- **Philosophical Problem**: Why the situation is fundamentally wrong — connects to the company's WHY.
- **Plan**: A simple 3-4 step process that reduces confusion and risk for the customer.
- **Direct CTA**: The primary action you want the customer to take (buy, schedule, sign up).
- **Transitional CTA**: A lower-commitment step for those not ready (download, subscribe, watch).
- **Stakes**: What happens if the customer doesn't act — the negative outcome.
- **Transformation**: The before-and-after change the customer experiences.
- **One-Liner**: The shortest version of the brand script: "[Customer] struggles with [problem]. [Company] provides [solution] so they can [success]."
- **Brand Script**: The complete 7-element StoryBrand narrative for a target segment.

## SOSTAC (Layer 4)

- **SOSTAC**: Situation, Objectives, Strategy, Tactics, Actions, Control — a complete planning methodology by PR Smith.
- **SWOT**: Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats — used here specifically for marketing capabilities, not general business.
- **SMART Objectives**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound goals.
- **5S Objectives** (PR Smith): Sell (grow revenue), Serve (add customer value), Sizzle (wow factor), Speak (two-way communication), Save (efficiency gains).

## RACE (Layer 5)

- **RACE**: Reach, Act, Convert, Engage — a customer lifecycle framework by Dave Chaffey (Smart Insights).
- **Reach**: Growing your audience online. Activities that drive awareness and traffic (SEO, PPC, social, PR, content).
- **Act**: Encouraging brand interactions and leads. The first meaningful engagement beyond a page visit.
- **Convert**: Driving online or offline sales. Moving from interested prospect to paying customer.
- **Engage**: Building customer loyalty and advocacy. Post-purchase relationship nurturing and referrals.

## PESO (Layer 6)

- **PESO**: Paid, Earned, Shared, Owned — a media categorization model by Gini Dietrich.
- **Paid Media**: Channels where you pay for placement — ads, sponsorships, influencer partnerships.
- **Earned Media**: Coverage you earn through PR, media relations, and word-of-mouth — articles, reviews, mentions.
- **Shared Media**: Social media and user-generated content — platforms where content is shared and amplified by others.
- **Owned Media**: Channels you fully control — website, blog, email list, app, podcast.

## Content Marketing Matrix (Layer 7)

- **Content Marketing Matrix**: A framework plotting content types across two axes: awareness-to-purchase and emotional-to-rational.
- **Entertain** (Emotional + Awareness): Content that attracts attention through enjoyment — quizzes, games, viral videos.
- **Inspire** (Emotional + Purchase): Content that drives purchase through emotional connection — testimonials, transformation stories, community.
- **Educate** (Rational + Awareness): Content that builds authority through information — guides, reports, infographics, articles.
- **Convince** (Rational + Purchase): Content that overcomes objections through evidence — case studies, demos, calculators, comparisons.

## Funnel Terminology

- **TOFU (Top of Funnel)**: Awareness stage. The customer is discovering a problem or encountering your brand for the first time.
- **MOFU (Middle of Funnel)**: Consideration stage. The customer is evaluating solutions and comparing options.
- **BOFU (Bottom of Funnel)**: Decision/Purchase stage. The customer is ready to buy and choosing a provider.
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: Total marketing and sales spend divided by number of new customers acquired.
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)**: Total revenue a customer generates over their entire relationship with the company.
- **CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)**: Cost to acquire one customer through a specific channel or campaign.
- **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**: Revenue generated per dollar spent on advertising.
- **CPL (Cost Per Lead)**: Cost to generate one lead through a specific channel or campaign.

## P&G Who/What/How (Layer 8)

- **WHO**: The target consumer — defined with precision (not just demographics but needs, occasions, and motivations).
- **WHAT**: The key benefit — the single most compelling reason to choose this product/brand.
- **HOW**: The delivery and communication method — how the benefit is delivered and how it is communicated to the target.
