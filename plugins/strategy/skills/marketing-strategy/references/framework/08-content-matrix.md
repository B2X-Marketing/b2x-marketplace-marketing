# Layer 7: Content Planning — Content Marketing Matrix

**Framework Source**: Content Marketing Matrix (Smart Insights / First10)
**Dependencies**: Layer 3 (StoryBrand brand scripts and messaging), Layer 5 (RACE journey stages and tactics), Layer 6 (PESO channel assignments)
**Produces**: Content mapped across four quadrants, content-to-RACE-to-PESO mapping, 90-day content calendar

---

## Why This Layer Matters

By this point you know WHO you are targeting (Layer 2), WHAT to say to them (Layer 3), WHERE they are in the journey (Layer 5), and WHICH channels to use (Layer 6). The missing piece is WHAT CONTENT to create — and the Content Marketing Matrix provides the organizing framework.

The matrix maps content across two axes:
- **Horizontal axis**: Awareness (left) to Purchase (right) — where the audience is in the buying journey
- **Vertical axis**: Emotional (top) to Rational (bottom) — the type of appeal the content uses

This creates four quadrants, each serving a different purpose and audience mindset. Most companies over-produce in one or two quadrants and neglect the others. A B2B company might produce endless whitepapers (Educate) but never create anything entertaining or inspiring. A consumer brand might entertain all day but never provide the rational proof needed to close the sale. The matrix forces balance.

## The Four Quadrants

### Quadrant 1: Entertain (Emotional + Awareness)

**Purpose**: Capture attention and build brand affinity. This content does not sell — it connects. It is designed to reach new audiences by being shareable, surprising, or delightful.

**Typical formats**:
- Quizzes and interactive assessments
- Games and challenges
- Viral videos and short-form video
- Branded entertainment content
- Memes and cultural commentary
- Behind-the-scenes content
- Competitions and contests
- Human interest stories

**RACE alignment**: Primarily **Reach** — gets you in front of new audiences.
**PESO alignment**: Primarily **Shared** (social platforms) and **Paid** (promoted/boosted content).

**Questions**:

1. **What entertaining content could you create that is genuinely fun or surprising for [Segment Name]?**
   - What does your audience find funny, interesting, or shareable?
   - What cultural moments, trends, or topics does your audience care about that you could participate in?
   - Can you create a quiz, assessment, or interactive tool that is both fun and relevant to your industry?

2. **What behind-the-scenes or human stories could you tell?**
   - Are there stories about your team, your process, or your culture that would resonate?
   - Can you show the human side of your company in a way that connects with your WHY from Layer 1?

3. **What is the brand voice for entertaining content?**
   - How far can you stretch from your core brand voice? (Humor, irreverence, playfulness — does it fit your brand?)
   - What are the boundaries? What would feel off-brand?

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If the user says "we're B2B, entertaining content doesn't work for us": "B2B buyers are still people. They scroll LinkedIn during lunch. They share interesting things with colleagues. What would make someone in your industry stop scrolling and say 'this is great'? That is entertainment in a B2B context."
- If ideas are too far from the brand: "Entertainment content still needs to connect to your brand. The test is: could people remember your brand after engaging with this? If it is funny but forgettable, it is not strategic."

### Quadrant 2: Inspire (Emotional + Purchase)

**Purpose**: Build trust and emotional connection that drives action. This content shows the transformation — what life looks like after working with you. It builds community and reinforces the decision to buy.

**Typical formats**:
- Customer testimonials and success stories (video and written)
- Community spotlights and features
- Reviews and ratings
- Live events and virtual experiences
- Celebrity or influencer endorsements
- User-generated content showcases
- Transformation stories (before/after)
- Mission-driven and purpose-led content (tied to WHY from Layer 1)

**RACE alignment**: Primarily **Convert** and **Engage** — builds the emotional confidence to buy and reinforces the decision post-purchase.
**PESO alignment**: **Earned** (testimonials, reviews), **Shared** (UGC, community), **Owned** (case study pages, testimonial sections).

**Questions**:

1. **What customer transformation stories could you tell?**
   - Who are your most successful customers? What was their "before and after"?
   - Reference the Success element of your brand script (Layer 3) — what does the transformation look like in real life?
   - Can you capture these stories in the customer's own words? (Video testimonials, written case studies, interview formats)

2. **How can you build community and belonging?**
   - Can you create a sense of identity around your customers? ("You are the kind of person/company who ___")
   - Can you host events (virtual or in-person) that bring customers together?
   - Can you feature customers in ways that make them feel valued and visible?

3. **What reviews, ratings, and social proof do you have — or need?**
   - Where do your customers leave reviews? (G2, Capterra, Google, Yelp, Trustpilot, industry-specific sites)
   - Do you have a systematic process for requesting reviews?
   - How do you showcase social proof on your website and marketing materials?

4. **What purpose-driven content connects to your WHY?**
   - Can you create content that shows how your company lives its WHY from Layer 1?
   - Are there cause-related or impact stories that would inspire your audience?

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If no customer stories exist: "Start collecting them now. Even one strong testimonial is worth more than ten blog posts. Identify your three happiest customers and ask: 'Would you be willing to share your experience?' Most will say yes if you make it easy."
- If inspiration feels forced: "Inspiration does not have to be a grand gesture. Showing a real customer who solved a real problem using your product — that is inspiring to someone who has the same problem. Keep it authentic."

### Quadrant 3: Educate (Rational + Awareness)

**Purpose**: Establish expertise and build trust through valuable information. This content answers questions, teaches concepts, and helps the audience understand their problem and the solution landscape. It is the engine of content marketing and SEO.

**Typical formats**:
- Blog articles and how-to guides
- Infographics and data visualizations
- Industry reports and original research
- Ebooks and comprehensive guides
- White papers and technical documentation
- Webinars and educational video series
- Podcasts and interview series
- Email courses and educational sequences
- Checklists, templates, and frameworks

**RACE alignment**: Primarily **Reach** (SEO-driven awareness content) and **Act** (lead magnets and gated educational content).
**PESO alignment**: **Owned** (blog, email, resource library), **Earned** (thought leadership, original research that gets cited), **Shared** (educational social posts).

**Questions**:

1. **What does [Segment Name] need to learn before they can buy?**
   - What misconceptions do they have about their problem or the solution space?
   - What questions do they ask during the awareness stage? (Reference search terms from Layer 5 Reach)
   - What would they need to understand to evaluate your solution properly?

2. **What educational content could you create that demonstrates expertise?**
   - What do you know that your audience does not — and would find valuable?
   - What frameworks, models, or approaches do you use that could be taught?
   - Can you create original research, surveys, or data analysis?
   - What are the "101" topics in your space that you could own?

3. **What lead magnets can you build from educational content?**
   - Which of these educational pieces could become gated content? (Reference Act stage from Layer 5)
   - What format is most valuable to this segment? (PDF guide, video series, template, tool, email course)
   - What topic would be compelling enough to earn an email address?

4. **What is your SEO and content marketing plan?**
   - What keyword clusters will you target? (From Layer 5 Reach and Layer 6 Owned)
   - What is the planned publishing cadence? (How many articles, guides, or content pieces per month?)
   - How will you promote educational content beyond your website? (Social sharing, email distribution, syndication)

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If the user only wants to create product content: "Product content serves people who already know they need your solution. Educational content reaches people who do not know you exist yet — they are searching for answers to their problems, not your product name. This is how you build the top of the funnel."
- If content ideas are too generic: "What topic could you create the definitive resource on? The piece so thorough that it becomes the go-to reference? That is the kind of educational content that compounds — it ranks in search, gets shared, gets cited, and positions you as the authority."
- If resources are limited: "Quality beats quantity. One comprehensive, well-researched piece per month is better than four shallow posts. What is the single most important topic you could own?"

### Quadrant 4: Convince (Rational + Purchase)

**Purpose**: Provide the logical proof and practical detail needed to close the sale. This is the content that the buyer uses to justify the purchase — to themselves, their boss, their procurement team. It answers "why you?" and "why now?" with evidence.

**Typical formats**:
- Case studies with specific metrics and results
- Product demos and walkthroughs
- ROI calculators and assessment tools
- Comparison and vs. pages
- Data sheets, spec sheets, and technical documentation
- Pricing pages and packages
- Webinars and product-focused presentations
- Free trials, pilots, and proof-of-concept offers
- FAQ pages and objection-handling content
- Analyst reports and third-party validation

**RACE alignment**: Primarily **Convert** — the content that tips the decision and supports the sales process.
**PESO alignment**: **Owned** (website, email nurture), **Earned** (analyst reports, awards, media validation), **Shared** (customer reviews and ratings on third-party platforms).

**Questions**:

1. **What proof do you have that your solution works?**
   - What case studies can you produce? For each: what was the problem, what did you do, and what were the measurable results?
   - What metrics can you share? (Revenue impact, time saved, cost reduced, efficiency gained)
   - Do you have any third-party validation? (Awards, certifications, analyst mentions, media coverage)

2. **What tools or content help the buyer justify the purchase?**
   - Can you create an ROI calculator or value assessment tool?
   - Do you have a comparison page showing how you differ from alternatives? (Reference positioning from Layer 2)
   - Is your pricing transparent and easy to understand?
   - Do you have content that helps the internal champion sell to their stakeholders? (Executive summary, one-pager, ROI deck)

3. **What are the most common rational objections, and what content addresses each?**
   - Reference the objection list from Layer 5 Convert stage
   - For each objection, what content format is most persuasive? (Case study, testimonial, data, demo, guarantee)

4. **What content supports the sales team in closing?**
   - Sales enablement materials: battle cards, one-pagers, proposal templates
   - Demo scripts and presentation decks
   - Reference customer contact list for prospect reference calls
   - Content for post-demo follow-up sequences

#### Follow-Up Probes

- If no case studies exist: "A case study is the single most powerful piece of conversion content. Even if you only have one, produce it thoroughly — with specific numbers, timeline, and customer quotes. Then build a process to create one per quarter."
- If the content is all features-focused: "Features tell, benefits sell, but results convince. Instead of listing what your product does, show what happened when a customer used it. Shift from 'our platform has X' to 'Customer Y achieved Z using our platform.'"
- If there is no sales enablement content: "Your sales team is creating their own materials — or going without. What are the three most common scenarios they face in sales conversations? Build a one-pager for each."

### Calendar Notes

- **Evergreen vs. timely**: Mark which pieces are evergreen (update periodically) vs. timely (tied to events, trends, seasons)
- **Repurposing plan**: Each major piece (blog post, webinar, case study) should be repurposed into 3-5 smaller pieces for social media
- **Production lead time**: Account for creation, review, and approval time — a case study typically takes 4-6 weeks from customer agreement to publication
- **Quarterly review**: At the end of 90 days, assess which quadrant and format performed best and adjust the next quarter's calendar
